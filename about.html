Jonghoon is a media artist who mainly focuses on new media. He creates hyper-realistic content that motivates the audience to participate in the artwork. He believes that participation and individual representation of the audience completes art entirely. From meaningless motions to intimate emotions, participants contribute to transforming the artwork into many different forms.

Our habitual and unintended motions can be recorded as data and processed into art. Jonghoon created experimental dance films by incorporating music and motion data of our daily behaviors and provided a new perspective to familiar movements. His recent work in the Silhak museum involved reconstructing a historical figure to 3D digital human using live motion capture data. His interactive artwork and motion data from the audience could revive the spirits of the past and bring the ancestors back to life in new forms.

He is also interested in manipulating physical data to create a new set of the universe. Utilizing Data visualization and Generative Art, he constructs a world that is built with different definitions and rules than those observed in the real world. The constructed universe evokes feelings of confusion and mystery which come from the gap between the two worlds.

His artwork continues to transform in search of his own “Crest Lines.” Unlike commercial films, his films are full of unexpected and montaged scenes that are hard to keep track of the sequence. The audience who are exposed to unpredicted storylines attempts to understand the film with personal experiences and prior knowledge which adds versatility to the film. He designs and plans crest lines in diverse forms of art, and they exhibit the importance of the participation of the audience in art.
